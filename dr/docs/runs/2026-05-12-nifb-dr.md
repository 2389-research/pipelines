Built a 32-sprint volunteer-management application end-to-end from spec, in one
workspace, with the `feature/composable-parts` branch of the dr/ pipelines. Final
state: **32 completed / 0 planned / 0 failed / 6 redecomposed** out of 38 ledger

LLM-level failure self-healed (scope-fence violation, two redecompositions, one
hung-process retry).

## What worked

### Composable parts paid off
The `implement_and_validate` and `write_and_validate` subgraphs ran cleanly across
multiple sprints and on the redecompose path (the latter exercised the validate-first
design of `write_and_validate` and validated proposals in 3–5 seconds with zero
rewrite cycles).

### Hardened scope-fence prompts (committed in `bd47209`)
Sprint 031, which had failed once with a scope-fence violation, passed cleanly on
retry. The commit message of the successful attempt explicitly documents the
cleanup: *"Removed scope-violating functions from prior attempt: getVolunteerHistory,
getPersonalisedRecommendations, needsWaiverReminder. Removed off-limits imports
(Registration model, Waiver service)."* That's exactly the behavior the prompt
changes were trying to drive. Across the rest of the run, no further scope-fence
violations were observed.

### Recovery primitives behaved correctly
- Sprint 021 (high complexity) failed review; RecoveryManager triaged to redecompose; splice ran; 3 child sprints completed cleanly.
- Sprint 027 (high complexity) failed review; same flow; 2 child sprints completed cleanly.
- Sprint 023 failed for technical reasons (hung worker); RecoveryManager correctly diagnosed it as a RETRY case, not redecompose; retry passed cleanly in 19 minutes.

The three-way distinction (retry / redecompose / escalate) RecoveryManager makes
worked exactly as designed.

### PlanManager held the right sprint at the right time
022 (the "Big Test" e2e sprint) was correctly held by PlanManager when its semantic
dependencies were still planned. The hold itself was correct — even though the
recovery flow it triggered ran on stale state and had to be killed.

## What required human intervention (and where the bugs live)

### ISSUE-001 — dep-graph back-edges (the dominant issue)

Three confirmed root-cause variants over today's run:

1. **Initial decomposition** generated back-edges: Sprint 020 ← Sprint 030, etc.
   The plan came out of `spec_to_sprints` with cycles already baked in. Triggered
   the first `deps_blocked_exit` and required surgery #1.

2. **Redecompose splice** introduces back-edges every time it runs. After 021 was
   redecomposed into 033/034/035, the splice updated sibling sprints (022, 023) to
   depend on the new children — a perfect back-edge. Same pattern when 027
   redecomposed: splice added 037 as a dep to 030/032/035/022/023. Required
   surgeries #2 and #3.

3. **Capstone auto-fix** is still suspected but not yet confirmed as a separate
   contributor (may or may not also be involved).

The accumulated cost: ~3 minutes of surgery per cycle, but **psychologically the
biggest gotcha** — every time it happens, you wonder if the run is actually
making progress or just going in circles.

### Stale redecompose-request replay

When tracker is killed mid-redecompose, the `.ai/redecompose-request.yaml` it
already wrote persists on disk. The next time the runner reaches `report_progress`
after a successful sprint completion, that stale request is picked up and replayed
— against a workspace state that has *moved on*. We hit this once today after
sprint 025 completed and the system tried to redecompose sprint 022 (which had
been resolved manually 30 minutes prior).

Fix candidates: (a) timestamp/run-id-stamp the request file and reject stale ones,
(b) clear it on tracker startup if it predates the current run, (c) clear it in
a SIGTERM handler.

### Misnumbered semantic ordering (related to ISSUE-001 but worth its own callout)

The initial decomposition put 022 and 023 (e2e test sprints) at IDs *lower* than
the feature sprints they're supposed to verify. This is a semantic mistake, not
purely a graph mistake — the LLM didn't think about "test sprints come last."
Fix candidates: stronger prompt direction to the decomposition agent that
*verification/test sprints must have the highest IDs in the plan*, OR add a
post-decomp pass that sorts sprints into a (feature → test) topological order.
